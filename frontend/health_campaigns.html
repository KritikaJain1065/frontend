Health Insurance

Ayushman Bharat Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY)

Target: Economically vulnerable families
Coverage: Up to ₹5 lakhs per family per year

Provides health insurance coverage for secondary and tertiary care hospitalization to poor and vulnerable families. It's the world's largest health insurance scheme fully financed by the government.

Key Benefits:

  • Cashless and paperless access to quality healthcare services
  • Covers hospitalization expenses including pre and post hospitalization
  • Includes 3 days of pre-hospitalization and 15 days post-hospitalization expenses
  • All pre-existing conditions covered from day one
  • No restriction on family size, age or gender

Eligibility:

Families identified based on deprivation and occupational criteria as per SECC database for rural and urban areas. The scheme covers approximately 10.74 crore poor and vulnerable families.

How to Apply:

Visit your nearest Ayushman Bharat Kendra, Common Service Centre (CSC), or follow these steps:

  1. Check eligibility by calling toll-free number 14555 or visiting PM-JAY portal
  2. Visit any empanelled hospital with required documents (Aadhaar card, ration card, etc.)
  3. Get verified and receive e-card

Healthcare Infrastructure

National Health Mission (NHM)

Target: All citizens with focus on rural areas
Type: Primary healthcare infrastructure

A flagship program launched by the Government of India to strengthen public health systems and provide accessible, affordable, and quality healthcare to rural and urban populations, with special focus on vulnerable groups.

Key Components:

  • National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) for rural areas
  • National Urban Health Mission (NUHM) for urban areas
  • Reproductive, Maternal, Newborn, Child and Adolescent Health (RMNCH+A)
  • Control of communicable and non-communicable diseases
  • Strengthening health systems and infrastructure

Services Available:

All citizens can access NHM services through public health facilities like Sub-Centers, Primary Health Centers (PHCs), Community Health Centers (CHCs), District Hospitals, and Urban Primary Health Centers.

How to Access:

Visit your nearest public health facility. For specific programs:

  • Dial 108 for emergency medical services
  • Contact local ASHA (Accredited Social Health Activist) worker for community-level services
  • Visit nearest health center for routine healthcare needs

Maternal Health

Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan (PMSMA)

Target: Pregnant women
Services: Free checkups on 9th of every month

A nationwide initiative to provide free comprehensive and quality antenatal care to pregnant women on the 9th of every month, ensuring healthy pregnancies and safe deliveries.

Key Services:

  • Free comprehensive antenatal check-ups by gynecologists/physicians
  • Essential pregnancy tests and screenings
  • Identification and management of high-risk pregnancies
  • Counseling on birth preparedness and complications
  • Early detection of pregnancy complications

Eligibility:

All pregnant women in their 2nd and 3rd trimesters. Special attention is given to high-risk pregnancies.

How to Access:

Visit the nearest government health facility (PHC, CHC, District Hospital) on the 9th of every month. You can also:

  • Register through local ASHA or ANM worker
  • Call the helpline at 104 for more information
  • Download the PMSMA app for registration and reminders

Child Health

Mission Indradhanush

Target: Children under 2 years & pregnant women
Focus: Complete immunization coverage

A nationwide immunization program aimed at protecting children against vaccine-preventable diseases. The mission aims to achieve full immunization coverage for all children and pregnant women.

Vaccines Provided:

  • Protection against 12 Vaccine-Preventable Diseases
  • Diphtheria, Whooping Cough, Tetanus, Polio
  • Tuberculosis, Hepatitis B, Meningitis
  • Pneumonia, Japanese Encephalitis, Measles, Rubella
  • Rotavirus diarrhea (in select states)

Eligibility:

All children under 2 years of age and pregnant women who have missed regular vaccinations.

How to Access:

Services are provided free of cost at government health facilities. To access:

  • Visit your nearest health center on immunization days
  • Contact local ASHA worker for information on upcoming immunization sessions
  • Call the National Health Helpline at 1800-180-1104 for details

Health Insurance

Rashtriya Swasthya Bima Yojana (RSBY)

Target: BPL families
Coverage: Up to ₹30,000 per family per annum

A health insurance scheme providing cashless coverage for hospitalization expenses to Below Poverty Line (BPL) families and other unorganized sector workers.

Key Benefits:

  • Cashless treatment at network hospitals
  • Coverage for most diseases requiring hospitalization
  • Transportation costs of up to ₹1,000 per year
  • Pre-existing conditions covered from day one
  • Smart card-based paperless and cashless transactions

Eligibility:

BPL families and workers in the unorganized sector as identified by the latest available BPL list. A premium of ₹30 per family per annum is charged at the time of enrollment.

How to Apply:

Enrollment is done through enrollment stations set up at various locations. To apply:

  1. Check if your name is in the BPL list with your local authorities
  2. Visit enrollment station with proof of identity and BPL status
  3. Pay ₹30 as registration fee
  4. Get photographed and biometric data collected
  5. Receive RSBY smart card

Traditional Medicine

National AYUSH Mission

Target: All citizens
Focus: Traditional healthcare systems

A centrally sponsored scheme to promote AYUSH (Ayurveda, Yoga, Unani, Siddha, and Homeopathy) medical systems through cost-effective AYUSH services and strengthening of educational systems.

Key Components:

  • Establishment of AYUSH hospitals and dispensaries
  • Supply of essential AYUSH medicines
  • Support for AYUSH educational institutions
  • Setting up of AYUSH wellness centers
  • Cultivation of medicinal plants

Services Available:

All citizens can access AYUSH services through AYUSH hospitals, dispensaries, and wellness centers across the country.

How to Access:

Visit your nearest AYUSH facility for consultations and treatments. To find AYUSH centers:

  • Check the directory on the AYUSH Ministry website
  • Contact local health department for AYUSH facilities in your area
  • Visit integrated AYUSH departments at district hospitals
